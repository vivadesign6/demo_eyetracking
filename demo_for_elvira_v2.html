Webcam Eye Tracking — Dissertation Prototype

This is a single-stimulus prototype demonstrating webcam-based eye tracking integrated into an e-commerce survey. It is part of the dissertation: "Designing for Shopping Motivations: How Hedonic and Utilitarian Shoppers Differ in Visual Attention to Product Design Elements in E-Commerce Interfaces."

Participants will view a product page image while the system records where they look, producing objective fixation data per AOI — Product Image, Brand, Description, and Price/Discount — without any specialist hardware.

  • 1
    Calibration — click 9 dots on screen while looking at each one (≈45 seconds)
  • 2
    Stimulus viewing — browse the product page naturally for up to 30 seconds
  • 3
    Results — see dwell time per AOI and the raw gaze coordinate log
Requirements: Desktop or laptop with a working webcam. Chrome or Firefox recommended. No video is recorded — only gaze coordinates (X, Y) are stored locally in this prototype.
